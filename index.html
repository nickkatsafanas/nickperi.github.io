WHAT'S A COMPUTER is an America rock opera influenced by the modern American operas of Robert Ashley (Private Parts, Improvement) and Ghostface Killah (R.A.G.U., Shakey Dog), the digressive novels of Austrian writer Thomas Bernhard (Concrete, Woodcutters), and the Rebetiko music of Greek singers Haris Alexiou and Vangelis Perpiniadis.

Join Giorgio Sardon as he sips an affordably priced beer in his rented Toulon, France cottage. With a handful of takeout menus before him, he strenuously contemplates what he might like for dinner, muttering in drone-like intonations as he makes this decision.
